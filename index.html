LETTER FROM THE SECRETARY GENERAL

“One of the great liabilities of history is that all too many people fail to remain awake through great periods of social change. Every society has its protectors of status quo and its fraternities of the indifferent who are notorious for sleeping through revolutions. Today, our very survival depends on our ability to stay awake, to adjust to new ideas, to remain vigilant and to face the challenge of change.” — Martin Luther King, Jr.
The world around us is being shaped with every fluctuation of the global economy, every oration of a policy statement and every negotiation of a treaty. In this dynamic geopolitical scenario, the only way for us to predict the future is for us to create it.

Dear Delegates,

I welcome you to the 5th edition of the Sanskriti Model United Nations Conference, 2016. Over the years, Sanskriti MUN has been attended by students from cities within India and across the world. It has become a platform for discussion of pertinent issues and expression of unique solutions. At Sanskriti, we believe that knowledge is liberation, and the conference embodies this belief because for these three days, you will cease to be just students. You will become change-makers, capable of transcending the realms of time and changing the course of history or of using your zeal and conviction to tackle the problems that plague our present society. You will have the power to form alliances, have your opinions heard and take decisions on matters ranging from drug trafficking in South America to the growing threat of the Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant. True learning lies in not merely accumulating a wealth of knowledge but in questioning what is perceived to be true and striving for improvement. The myriad problems that we face will not be easy to overcome; however, I believe that our generation, with its insatiable desire to bring about change and its tenacious faith in the power of ideas, is capable of making the world a better place. Intellectually stimulating activities such as MUNs give students the opportunity to step into the shoes of policy makers and practise the art of diplomacy. I hope that over the course of the conference you will be able to form new perspectives on issues through articulation, participate enthusiastically in discussion of the riveting topics that have been selected, refine your debating skills and make new friendships. I look forward to seeing you in April.

Suhasini Vira

Secretary General

Sanskriti Model United Nations, 2016.

LETTER FROM THE DIRECTOR GENERAL OF GENERAL ASSEMBLIES


Dear delegates,

It is my esteemed honour and immense pleasure to invite you to the 5th session of Sanskriti Model United Nations 2016, held at Sanskriti School, New Delhi, India, from the 28th to the 30th of April.

At Sanskriti, we believe that ‘knowledge is liberation’, and it is this resolute belief that encourages us to bring to you a MUN conference that will open your eyes to issues of global significance with renewed perspective. We, usually, view the problems of the world from a distance that prevents us from absorbing their momentousness; however, when we are introduced to the same conflicts from greater proximity, we begin to view them with more relevance. SMUN endeavours to show you the world from the eyes of a world leader who must engage in insightful debate and discussion, fruitful collaboration and the art of diplomacy to sow the seeds of peace and prosperity.

With 9 dynamic committees, students from schools both within the country and abroad, and a committed secretariat, SMUN represents a unique opportunity for delegates to express their views, while at the same time, explore new and varied ideas, to alleviate dilemmas plaguing the world.

Through your enthusiastic participation, we hope to make SMUN a memorable and enriching learning experience that will challenge you to work towards change both in and outside committee while grappling with the multifaceted problems of the world.

We sincerely hope that you will consider joining us for Sanskriti Model United Nations 2016. We look forward to meeting you this April. Please feel free to contact us with any queries regarding the conference.

Warm regards,

Anindini Singh

Director General

Sanskriti Model United Nations, 2016
